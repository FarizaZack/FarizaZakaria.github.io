About

I started out in finance and accounting. A lot of my early work was the usual routine: reports, reconciliations, and month-end tasks that came around again and again.

After a while I got curious about the repetitive parts. If I was copying the same numbers every month, there was probably a simpler way to do it, so I started looking for one.

That's how I got into data analytics and automation. It usually starts with a finance problem, like a report that takes too long or numbers that don't reconcile. The tools are just how I solve it, mostly SQL, Power BI or Tableau, and a bit of Python.

These days I mostly build small internal tools. Nothing fancy. Just practical things that save people time, cut down on manual work, and actually get used.
